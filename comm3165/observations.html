Systematic observation of individuals in context.

Pros Cons
Good for poorly understood topics Bad for large samples since collection takes time
Shows what people actually do without interruption of bias or recall Benefits from being face to face since people don’t like being recorded
Gives insight into behavior Won’t tell us what users are thinking only what they’re doing

Frameworks

Doing observation without a framework can result in too much data being generated. We use frameworks to narrow our focus.

Person, Place & Thing

  • Who is using it?
  • Where are they using it?
  • What are they doing?

Problem Focused

A goal oriented framework where we look at what’s causing problems and how users work around it.

Detailed Context

Used when other frameworks are lacking since it can produce a lot of data.

Diaries

Diaries feature people reflecting on their experiences at some regular interval.

  • Named because informants used to actually record data into a written diary, which was given to researchers at the end of the study.
Pros Cons
Good with scattered participants Relies on participant honesty
Good when the activity is private Participants will procrastinate completing their journal
Low time requirement Participant memory is fuzzy
Inexpensive  

Experience Sampling Method (ESM)

“in the moment diary”

Essentially just a diary but instead of journaling at a regular time, participants are prompted randomly.

  • Prompt users randomly for an immediate response
  • Addresses memory weakness of diaries

Interaction Logs + Analytics

Passive recordings of user actions in an already existing application.

  • Presents some ethical concerns about privacy
  • Requires data expertise to analyze due to the sheer volume
  • Some data is publicly accessible
