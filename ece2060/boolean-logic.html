Laws of Boolean Algebra

Distributive

Ordinary Distributive Law

\(X (Y + Z) = XY + XY\) This one is very similar to actual algebra.

Second Distributive Law

\(X + YZ = (X+Y)(X+Z)\) This one does not work in real algebra but it does with boolean algebra.

Duality

Given a boolean expression, change all 1’s to 0’s and vice versa Change all Ands to Ors and vice versa. Keep all variables and their complements the same.

DeMorgan’s Laws

These laws were created by DeMorgan after Boole and deal with distributing complements. These laws follow the general pattern of:

  1. Calculate the duality expression
  2. Using the Dual, complement each literal

First Form

\(\overline{X + Y} = \overline{X}\cdot \overline{Y}\)

Second Form

Kind of an inverse of the first. \(\overline{X \cdot Y} = \overline{X} + \overline{Y}\)

\(\overline{G} = A\overline{C} D+ \overline{B}C\) find \(G\)

Show Answer

\begin{align*} \overline{G} &= A\overline{C} D+ \overline{B}C \\ \overline{\overline{G}} &= \overline{A\overline{C} D+ \overline{B}C} \\ G &= \overline{(A\overline{C}D)}\overline{\overline{B}C} \\ \end{align*}

From here we can simplify the expression further.

Functional Completeness

We can represent any boolean expression we just need the Not, And and Or operations. However we can do the same the inverses Not, Nand and Nor.

  • Furthermore with DeMorgan’s Laws we can make an Or gate with just Not and And.

Minterms

A minterm(\(m_n\)) of \(n\) variables is a product of \(n\) literals. That means that:

  • Each variable appears once, either complemented or not.
  • Both the complement and the true from cannot appear together.

Summation notation can be used for a combination of Minterms

\begin{align*} f(A, B, C) &= \sum m(3, 4, 5, 6, 7)\\ &= m_3 + m_4 + m_5 + m_6 + m_7 \end{align*}

Karnaugh Maps

Karnaugh Maps are used to look at minterms and get the sum of products without doing the boolean algebra.

  • When we go from one cell to an adjacent one, we can only change one variable.
