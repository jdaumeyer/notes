User Centered Design
COMM3545 with Jesse Fox

Users
Humans that directly interact with the product that we’re designing
Stakeholders
Humans that are effected by the product who don’t use said product

Usability

Usability
is the objective list of features that a design needs in order to be functional
User Experience
is the subjective feelings of a user while they use a product.
  • Best when gathering user data with every design iteration.
User-Centered Design (Human-Centered Design)
A design framework that prioritizes the needs of the users
The Problem Space
The issue being solved and all related external factors. Broken into the components, restrictions and possible solutions.
Problem Statement
A summary of the problem, the more specific the better.

Usability Goals

Functionality

Does the product do what it was designed to do quickly? Does it have effectiveness, utility, efficiency and usefulness.

Visibility

The user should be able to find all of the functions.

  • A design with good Visibility has good affordances and feedback

Consistency

Similar elements should look the same or be related in order to create a good mental model.

Functional Consistency
Elements work the same.
Aesthetic Consistency
Elements look the same/similar.
Internal Consistency
Features are the same across a brand.
External Consistency
Features match the rest of the world.

Simplicity

Things that are simple have higher learnability and memorability.

Accessibility

Individual Barriers
Understandability
rely on simple language and existing mental models to avoid cognitive barriers.
Perceptibility
can people perceive what’s going on to avoid sensory barriers.
Operability
is if possible for everyone to use regardless of physical barriers.
Affordability
Can people actually buy your product?
Contextual Barriers
Adaptability
Can the product be used in different contexts
Compatability
Can the product work with existing tech?
Availability
Is it even possible to acquire a product?

Safety

Does the product protect uses from misuse and errors. Done through Constraints that limit behavior.

Forgiveness & Recovery
allow users to undo mistakes
Factors of Safety
Many layers of safety
Modularity
components should serve a specific purpose and be replaceable.

Flexibility

Allow users (appropriate) control and customization over a product.

Multi-Layer Design
Different layers of a product within one product so that basic and advanced users can use the same product at different levels of competence.
