The Factors

Charisma is something that is made up of factors. It’s not just a stat that you’re born with.

Authority

Authority is power to reward or punish people

  • Milgram experiments show how much authority can persuade someone.

Credibility

Credibility is how much you trust someone. It is not based in an objective reality, it’s all about perception

To maximize Credibility we want to establish 3 characteristics:

Expertise
Show the audience you know what you’re talking about, be it through experience.
Trustworthiness
Perceived honesty; showing that you will tell the truth.
Goodwill
Perceived caring; showing that you have good intentions for the people you’re talking too.

These will not happen naturally, we need to put effort into them. However, they aren’t always important. There are 3 audience factors that influence credibility:

Audience Size
Larger audiences require more energy and volume that would be inappropriate for a small one.
Communicator Role
Different roles for different settings. Don’t be casual in a professional setting.
Cultural Dynamics
Things that are polite in one culture can be rude in another.

Expectations

Often meeting speaking expectations will increase credibility however there can be times where breaking expectations can increase credibility as well.

  • Being vegan, people expect you to come off as militant but not coming off that way can lead to a conversation. i.e. telling someone how they’re wrong (even if you’re right) can casue a divide.
  • Presumption that a speaker’s background prevents objectivity

Attractiveness

Attractiveness is how much you relate to someone

Likability
Speakers are more likely to be credible if they make people feel good.
Similarity
Speakers that share values and perspectives with their audiences are more convincing.
Appearance
People pay more attention to physically attractive people and it becomes associated with the message.
  • Works better when the audience doesn’t already have a strong attitude
